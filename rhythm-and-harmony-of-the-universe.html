RHYTHM AND HARMONY OF THE UNIVERSE

Mon 07 January 2019
By Aniket
Category: Blog

What happens if one evening you suddenly start hearing the sound of stars and planets? Well, it’s quite obvious that it won’t be pleasurable to hear such earth-shattering sounds coming from countless stars altogether. But it’s not true. Actually, we are constantly hearing the sound of the universe. The reason why we don’t hear anything is that we don’t know what it’s like to not hear. The same way we are not able to feel the atmospheric pressure because we don’t know what it’s like to not feel. However, we can understand the rhythm and harmony of the universe with the help of mathematical equations. Music and Astronomy There is a significant connection between music and astronomy. Music illuminates the study of astronomy, while astronomy provides a context for understanding music. There is geometry in the humming of the strings, there is music in the spacing of the spheres Pythagoras Musica Universalis Musica Universalis is an ancient concept devised by Pythagoras ( most famous for his ‘Pythagoras theorem’) and his followers. It states that proportions in the movements of celestial bodies are in the form of music. This “music” is not usually thought to be literally audible, but a harmonic, mathematical or religious concept.One day Pythagoras listened to the sound of hammers coming from a forge. The sound produced by hammers were harmonious. Therefore, he examined the hammers and found that the hammers which were harmonious with each other shared a relationship in their respective weight, their weights were in a simple fraction. Thus, he concluded that 1:2 ratios produced an ‘octave’ with a higher pitch and different ratios produced different harmonies. Moreover, he stated that objects produce sound in motion. As planets and celestial bodies are in motion, therefore, they also produce sound resulting in a harmony called ” Music of Spheres”. As these sounds are continuously sounding, therefore, we don’t know how it feels to hear. Therefore we can’t distinguish it from our known idea of silence. Harmonic Law In 17th century, Kepler with the help of platonic geometry determined the distances between planets and refined harmonics of the universe which ultimately resulted in the Kepler’s 3rd law of Planetary motion ( also called as Harmonic Law) According to the law, The squares of the orbital periods of the planets around the Sun are proportional to the cubes of the orbital semi-major axes. Kepler’s third law provides an accurate description of the period and distance for a planet’s orbits about the sun. Additionally, the same law that describes the T2/R3ratio for the planets’ orbits about the sun also accurately describes the T2/R3 ratio for any satellite (whether a moon or a man-made satellite) about any planet. String Theory One of the main objective of Physics is to find a Single Theory which unifies all forces ( Gravity, Electromagnetism, Weak and Strong Nuclear force). In order to find the ” Theory of Everything ” a lot of interesting ideas and new theories came up. Some of the best theories till now are – String theory Loop Quantum Theory Casual Dynamic Triangulations Quantum Einstein Gravity Quantum Graphity Internal Relativity E8 String Theory is probably the best known theory of everything. In string theory, quarks and electron are visualized as sub-microscopic “strings” that vibrate (much like on a musical instrument) rather than visualizing the smallest particles of matter as minuscule points. In other words, fundamental particles we observe are not actually particles at all, but tiny strings that only “look” like particles to scientific instruments because they are so small. From Pythagoras to string theory, surprisingly, everything indicates the strong connections between music and physics. We can convert the motion of planets into musical rhythm and harmony by speeding and bringing the orbital frequencies into human hearing range. The Musical K2–138 K2–138 is one of the most musical solar systems ever found. It has 5 inner and 1 outer planet. The inner 5 planets are very close to 3:2 resonances (musicians call it’s a perfect 5th) and follow the tuning system designed by Pythagoras himself in which all notes are related by a perfect 5th and due to this simple pattern, each pair plays the interval of a perfect 5th (the first two notes of ‘Twinkle, Twinkle, little star’).
